Congresswoman Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (D-NY)

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, youngest woman to hold office in United States Congress, is a staunch champion for basic human and environmental rights issues

Facts

  • Born October 13, 1989
  • US Representative for New York's 14th congressional district
  • Youngest woman ever to serve in US Congress
  • Double-majored in international relations and economics from Boston University, graduating cum laude
  • Member of "The Squad" along with Reps. Ihlan Omar, Ayanna Pressley, and Rashida Tlaib, who represent the democratic socialist, minority bloc of the House of Representatives
  • Previously worked as a waitress and bartender
  • In a domestic partnership with her boyfriend, web developer Riley Roberts
  • Guest-judged an episode of RuPaul's Drag Race in 2020

Political Positions

  • Environment - climate change "is the single biggest national security threat for the United States and the single biggest threat to worldwide industrialized civilization"; co-authored and advocates for the Green New Deal
  • Immigration - proponent of defunding and abolishing US Immigration and Customs Enforcement agency; after a tour of ICE detention centers, she responded with condemnation, calling them "concentration camps"
  • Health care - believes access to health care is a basic human right and supports legislation for Medicare for All
  • Education - supports student loan debt forgiveness and reforming the broken education system to make it affordable for all
  • LGBTQIA+ issues - supports expansion of equality measures and elimination of discrimination against LGBTQ+ persons in every facet of society
